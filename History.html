History

In the year of 1947, the Association for Computing Machinery or the ACM – was established to unite computing professionals and the like around the world, with its sole purpose being to “advance the science, development, construction and application of the new machinery for computing, reasoning, and other handling of information”.

ACM in the 1947s

ACM was founded after the first creation of the ENIAC – the first programmable, electronic and general-purpose computer, proving their promptness to serve the public good with its official standards and rules of ethics. ACM supported it’s profession with its leadership skills and the opportunities granted by their cooperation, strengthening it’s members professional growth within the highest standards and with recognitions of it’s well deserved associates.

In the present time, subsequent committees for the ACM finalized its mission in a more specific manner: “to advance the art, science, engineering, and application of information technology, serving both professional and public interests by fostering the open interchange of information and by promoting the highest professional and ethical standards” with this, ACM is able to cater many aspirants, hobbyists, and computing professionals all over the world with a reminder of its rule of ethics and a framework of its standards through quality and security – ensuring that all those who have a particular interest in the field contribute for the better light of the community and in advancing the future of science.
